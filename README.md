## Artifacts

### Week 1 — Evaluating Resources

#### [Tool & Resource Evaluation Checklists](./checklists/)

Two interactive evaluation checklists built as a single GitHub Pages–hosted HTML tool:

**Online Tools Evaluation Checklist** — Pre-adoption framework for evaluating digital tools, applications, and platforms before recommending to faculty. Eight criteria drawn from course readings and institutional practice:

1. Instructional Alignment & Value
2. Affordability & Total Cost of Ownership
3. Redundancy Check *(institutional addition)*
4. Vendor Stability & Ownership
5. Instructor Lift
6. Strategic Plan Alignment *(institutional addition)*
7. Canvas Integration
8. Student Learning Outcomes

**OER & Online Resources Evaluation Checklist** — Framework for evaluating open educational resources, articles, videos, and online course materials before sharing with faculty or including in course design. Eight criteria:

1. Authority & Credibility
2. Accuracy & Evidence
3. Currency
4. Relevance & Course Alignment
5. Purpose, Perspective & Bias
6. Accessibility & Usability
7. Open Licensing & Permissions
8. Adaptability & Practical Usability

**Features:** Live scoring with color-coded feedback, notes field per criterion, summary text output, recommendation decision, reset function.

**Sources synthesized:**
- Bovard, B. *Online Technology Tool Selection Criteria*
- Anstey, L. & Watson, G. (2018). *A Rubric for Evaluating E-Learning Tools in Higher Education.* EDUCAUSE Review.
- Workforce EdTech. *Tool Evaluation Criteria.* Ed Tech Center @ World Education.
- Jefferson College Library. *Evaluating AI Tools: VALID-AI and ROBOT Test.*
- ASCCC. (2024). *Evaluating Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools in an Academic Setting.*
- SCC Strategic Plan FY2025–2028.

## Context

I'm rebuilding SCC's Adjunct & Faculty Info Portal — a Canvas-based resource repository that has been my primary faculty support infrastructure for the past several years. This course is running in direct parallel with that project, so every artifact I build here is designed to be immediately deployable, not theoretical.

The two evaluation checklists were built around the questions I actually ask myself during vendor evaluation at a small, resource-constrained community college: *Will we use this? Can we afford it? Do we already have something? Who owns it? How much does this add to faculty workload? Does it map to our strategic priorities? Does it work in Canvas? Does it improve what students learn?*
