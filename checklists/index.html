Online Tools Evaluation Checklist

For evaluating digital tools, applications, and platforms before adoption or recommendation to faculty. Rate each criterion 2 (meets), 1 (partially meets), or 0 (does not meet).

1

Instructional Alignment & Value

Will we actually use this? Does it directly address an identified need, or is it a solution looking for a problem?
Sources: EDUCAUSE Rubric (Functionality/Pedagogical Alignment); ASCCC AI Evaluation Framework (Pedagogical Alignment)
2

Affordability & Total Cost of Ownership

Can we afford this — now, and as we scale? What is the full cost picture?
Sources: Workforce EdTech (Affordability); Bovard (Access — cost); ASCCC (Cost and Licensing)
3

Redundancy Check

Do we already have something that does this? Is this filling a genuine gap or duplicating an existing tool?
Source: Institutional practice — added from active vendor evaluation experience
4

Vendor Stability & Ownership

Who owns this? Is the company stable? What happens if they’re acquired, pivot, or shut down?
Sources: Jefferson College ROBOT Test (Owner); Workforce EdTech (Longevity); Bovard (Access — longevity)
5

Instructor Lift

How much does this add to a faculty member’s job? Is the learning curve reasonable for adjuncts with limited time?
Sources: Bovard (Workload & Time Management); EDUCAUSE (Teaching Presence/Facilitation); Workforce EdTech (User Support)
6

Strategic Plan Alignment

How does this connect to SCC’s FY2025–2028 Strategic Priorities? Can we make the case to leadership?
Source: SCC Strategic Plan FY2025–2028 — institutional addition not in readings
7

Canvas Integration Revised

Does this work seamlessly within our LMS (Learning Management System), or does it create friction for students and faculty? Canvas is SCC’s LMS.
Sources: EDUCAUSE Rubric (LMS Integration); Bovard (Workload — embeddability) | Revised: added plain-language glosses on LTI, iframe, and API terminology per peer feedback (Week 1 discussion)
8

Student Learning Outcomes Revised

How does this improve what students actually learn? Does it support higher-order thinking or just digitize busywork? Does it serve all students equitably?
Sources: Workforce EdTech (Proven Effectiveness); ASCCC (Pedagogical Alignment, Student Engagement); EDUCAUSE (Cognitive Presence) | Revised: added explicit UDL framework reference and equity/diverse learners language — self-identified gap in Week 1 reflection
9

Equity, Belonging & Representation New

Does this tool reflect and serve our student population? Are diverse voices and perspectives centered in its design, content, and use cases?
Sources: ASCCC (Bias and Fairness, Support for Diverse Needs); EDUCAUSE (UDL, Accessibility Standards) | Added: self-identified gap noted in Week 1 reflection; reinforced by peer feedback in Week 1 discussion

OER & Online Resources Evaluation Checklist

For evaluating open educational resources, articles, videos, and online course materials before sharing with faculty or including in course design. Rate each criterion 2 (meets), 1 (partially meets), or 0 (does not meet).

1

Authority & Credibility

Who created this and why should we trust them? Is the author or organization clearly identified and credible?
Sources: ASCCC (Ethical Use/Transparency); Workforce EdTech (Proven Effectiveness)
2

Accuracy & Evidence

Are the claims accurate and supported by evidence? Has this been reviewed or verified?
Sources: Jefferson College VALID-AI (Accuracy Check); ASCCC (Bias and Fairness)
3

Currency Revised

Is this current enough for the subject? In fast-moving fields, when was this last updated — and when should it be re-evaluated?
Sources: Workforce EdTech (Longevity); Bovard (Workload — sustainability) | Revised: added iterative re-evaluation framing per Mariette’s Week 3 feedback on treating OER adoption as an ongoing process rather than a one-time decision
4

Relevance & Course Alignment

Does this match the course outcomes, student level, and learning context where it will be used?
Sources: EDUCAUSE (Instructional Alignment); ASCCC (Pedagogical Alignment)
5

Purpose, Perspective & Representation Revised

Who made this and why? What perspective is centered, and what’s missing? Whose voices and experiences are reflected — or absent?
Sources: Jefferson College VALID-AI (Diversity and Bias); ASCCC (Bias and Fairness) | Revised: renamed from “Purpose, Perspective & Bias”; added authorship diversity and student experience representation language — self-identified gap in Week 1 reflection
6

Accessibility & Usability Revised

Can all students access and use this — including students with disabilities, students on mobile devices, and students with limited bandwidth?
Sources: EDUCAUSE (Accessibility Standards, UDL); Bovard (Access — ADA); ASCCC (Support for Diverse Needs) | Revised: added explicit UDL framework reference, low-bandwidth access consideration, and mobile compatibility — self-identified gap in Week 1 reflection
7

Open Licensing & Permissions

Can we actually use, share, or adapt this? What does the license allow?
Sources: EDUCAUSE (Privacy, Data Protection & Rights — IP); Bovard (Privacy & IP)
8

Adaptability & Practical Usability

Can this be revised, downloaded, or shared in useful formats? Is it truly no-cost for students?
Sources: EDUCAUSE (Archiving, Saving & Exporting); Bovard (Privacy & IP — archival); Workforce EdTech (Affordability)
