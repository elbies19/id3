UDOIT — Check Your Course’s Accessibility
As part of our institutional commitment to accessibility and Universal Design for Learning (UDL), Salem Community College has partnered with CidiLabs to offer its UDOIT Accessibility Checker tool to our instructors in Canvas. This course-level tool allows you to check the accessibility of your course content in real time — and quickly fix most issues that may arise. UDOIT’s checked criteria are mapped directly to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) criteria.
What is UDOIT?

UDOIT — pronounced “You Do It” — stands for the Universal Design Online Inspection Tool. UDOIT is a course-level accessibility checker that scans online course content, identifies possible accessibility issues, and guides course designers and instructors in repairing those issues using inclusive design principles — all from within the UDOIT interface.

SCC Teaching Tip
UDOIT should be available to you in the left-hand navigation of your Canvas course as “UDOIT Cloud”. If you do not see UDOIT available in each of your classes, contact the Academic Technology Team at lschwartz@salemcc.edu.
What does UDOIT look for?

UDOIT is configured to identify a wide variety of potential accessibility issues in Canvas content, including but not limited to:

  • Insufficient color contrast with backgroundText color should be easily viewable and should not be the only indicator of meaning or function. Color balance should have at least a 4.5:1 ratio for small text and 3:1 ratio for large text.
  • Using color for text without additional emphasis (e.g. bold or italics)Color can enhance content aesthetics, but it shouldn’t be the only way of conveying meaning. Use color with another visual cue like bold text, icons, or descriptive wording to ensure users with color blindness or screen readers can understand the emphasis.
  • Missing or insufficient alt text for uploaded imagesAlternative Text (Alt Text) is a non-visual way to describe the meaning of an image. Provide a brief description for screen reader users. It should not be the image file name and should be 150 characters or less.
  • Lack of captions in video contentInclude captions with all video content to ensure accessibility for users who are deaf or hard of hearing. Captions should cover dialogue, sound effects, and speaker identification.
  • Auto-generated captions in video contentAuto-generated captions are often inaccurate. For educational content, use accurate, manually reviewed captions. We highly recommend using Canvas Studio for video content — it provides auto-generated captions and an easy interface for manual review.
  • Skipped heading levels or confusing page structureHeadings should follow a clear hierarchy. Start with H2 in course content and move to deeper levels (H3, H4, etc.) as you create subsections. Avoid skipping heading levels to help screen readers and all users navigate your content.
  • Nondescript link text (e.g. “click here”)Use descriptive link text that clearly identifies the content being linked to (e.g., “Class Schedule” instead of “Click Here” or “schedule.html”).
SCC Teaching Tip
A full list of accessibility criteria evaluated in UDOIT can be found in the UDOIT User Guide. As WCAG criteria evolve, these may change and/or result in changes to your overall course accessibility score. Contact the Academic Technology Team for guidance.
How do I use UDOIT to scan my course?

When selected from the Canvas course menu, UDOIT will automatically scan a course once per day. You can scan a course multiple times per day by selecting the “Scan Now” button in the upper right corner.

The first time you access the tool, UDOIT will scan all of the content in your course. This full scan may take a few minutes to complete. In subsequent uses, UDOIT will only scan new and updated content to complete the process more efficiently.

SCC Teaching Tip
Plan to initiate an initial UDOIT scan in your course(s) at the beginning of the semester. Your first scan may take some time to complete. Contact the Academic Technology Team if your scan does not complete or if you encounter any issues.
What does my UDOIT score mean?

UDOIT provides instructors with several different scores, broadly separated into your overall course score and your category scores.

  • Overall course scoreAn aggregated weighted percentage that takes into account: how many accessibility issues are present, the severity of any identified issues, and the level of potential impact to students.
  • Category scoresA breakdown of identified issues in three categories: High Impact (60% of your overall score), Medium Impact (30%), and Low Impact (10%).

UDOIT utilizes a weighted rubric to identify accessibility issues in descending order of severity. This rubric is customized to SCC and allows us to identify the most common issues in our courses while giving instructors the opportunity to quickly remedy the most easily-fixed issues.

Currently, UDOIT is configured to scan unpublished content in a given course in addition to all student-facing material. While this may lower your overall score, we have chosen to enable this feature to encourage instructors to cull outdated material. In many cases — especially if you’ve been copying over a course for multiple semesters — you may find it easier to simply delete old, unused material.

SCC Teaching Tip
Don’t panic if you do not receive a perfect score on your first scan! The first number you receive is a preliminary score for your reference and should be seen as a guide for improvement rather than an evaluation of your course material.
How do I fix issues UDOIT identifies?

UDOIT includes a resolution tool for identified issues called UFIXIT, available upon completion of your first course-level scan. UFIXIT classifies identified issues within three categories:

StatusWhat it means
⚠ Error Active issues with accessibility that must be resolved for your course to be compliant with WCAG standards. These have the greatest impact on both the student experience and your overall UDOIT score.
ⓘ Suggestion Higher-level accessibility issues that, while still impacting the student experience and your score, may be more nuanced and require closer examination.
✓ Fixed / Resolved An identified issue that has been corrected within UFIXIT or manually and is no longer considered an error or suggestion within UDOIT.

UDOIT also identifies issues in two categories useful to instructors: Easiest to fix and High Impact Issues. These categories are available under the “Ways to Get Started” pane on your UDOIT dashboard after completing your first course scan.

  • Easiest to fixAccessibility items that take only a few moments to resolve. These may include increasing color contrast or providing descriptive alt text for an image.
  • High Impact IssuesThe most critical issues identified within your course, such as links lacking descriptive text, color used alone to communicate emphasis, or video captions that are auto-generated.

While you should plan to resolve all issues identified in UDOIT over time, these two categories provide a useful starting point as you begin working toward making your course more accessible.

When is it okay to “Manually Resolve” an issue?

As you are resolving issues with UDOIT, in some cases an issue cannot be fixed within the tool itself. When this happens, you have the option to use the “Manual Resolution” feature. It is important to know when it is appropriate to do so.

When manual resolution is appropriate:
  • YouTube Captions Scanning Unavailable Although UDOIT can scan videos hosted on YouTube, the YouTube API only allows a certain number of scans per day. Sometimes the API key runs out of credits before the end of the day, resulting in this error.
    NOTE: This error should only be manually resolved if you have watched the video and determined that the captions are both present and accurate.
  • Closed Captions Were Auto-Generated When a video is uploaded from YouTube, Kaltura, or Vimeo, UDOIT can detect if captions were auto-generated. We strongly suggest you return to the video where it is hosted, adjust the captions as needed, and return to the UDOIT tool. Once captions are accurate, you can select “Scan Video For Caption Updates” or use Manual Resolution.
  • “False positives” identified by UDOIT When scanning for accessibility issues, UDOIT errs on the side of caution and may occasionally flag issues that don’t actually need to be addressed. For example, it may flag text with a dark gray color that isn’t actually being used to signal meaning or emphasis.
    Once you have used your human judgment to determine that an issue is a false positive and is not, in fact, inaccessible, using Manual Resolution will remove it from future scans.
When you should NOT manually resolve an issue:
  • Alt text missing from images For these issues, you can either provide a description of the image if it is important for learners to understand the content, or mark the image as decorative if it is not relevant to the page’s content.
    Manually resolving this issue does not provide proper context for screen readers and should not be used.
  • Avoid using color alone for emphasis (when it’s genuinely an issue) If text actually is emphasized using color alone, this is not a false positive. Resolve it in UFIXIT by adding bolding, italicizing the text, or removing the styling altogether.
    Manual resolution does not resolve the issue and therefore should not be used.
SCC Teaching Tip
When in doubt, err on the side of caution and do not mark an issue as manually resolved unless you’re absolutely sure the content in question is accessible. Contact the Academic Technology Team if you’re unable to fix an identified issue in UFIXIT or are unsure whether specific content can be manually resolved.
