Getting Started
Using the Instructor Checklist
In each course, you will see the SCC Instructor Checklist in your left-hand Canvas navigation. This checklist features four key items — our Core Four — that will ensure that you (and your students!) are prepared to start the semester off right, even before it begins.
What is the Instructor Checklist? Where can I find it?

The Instructor Checklist is a tool in Canvas designed to help you keep track of key action items we ask our instructors here at SCC to complete prior to the start of class.

You can locate the Instructor Checklist by selecting ReadyGO Instructor in the left-hand course navigation of your Canvas course shell.

We know you have plenty on your plate already, so we've kept these to a minimum: what we call our Core Four Items.

SCC Teaching Tip

What are the Core Four Items?

The Core Four items are designed with best practices and Caring Campus Behavioral Commitments in mind to ensure a productive start for you and your students right at the beginning of the term. Select any item below to learn more.

Core Four — Item 1

Your course syllabus addendum provides critical course information students may (and should!) reference throughout the semester. This should include at a bare minimum:

  • Course number and section
  • Instructor contact information
  • Textbook and other required course materials
  • Grading policies and criteria
  • Instructor and course policies
  • Course outline/tentative schedule

The syllabus addendum is your contract with your students about how your class will be structured, what the semester will look like, how to contact you, and how they'll be assessed. A clear, well-structured syllabus serves as a day-one guidemap and sets the tone for a successful semester.

A well-constructed syllabus addendum is also one of the six behavioral commitments from Caring Campus, specifically Commitment #3: Comprehensive Syllabus. For more information, see pages 33–34 in the SCC Faculty Handbook.

You don't have to start from scratch! We offer several syllabus templates, both for Canvas and for printable handouts.

Sample syllabi for reference:

  • HUM101 Introduction to the Humanities
  • ENG102 English Composition II
  • ENG202 Introduction to Speech Communication

Templates and samples are available in your Canvas course shell.

Core Four — Item 2

Not all assignments are created equal — weighted assignment groups allow you to set specific percentages for different types of assignments. You can have as many or as few of these as you wish, but the categories and their associated weights should match those outlined in your syllabus addendum.

Weighting your grades properly ensures that your overall course grades are fair, accurate, and reflective of student work.

With weighted assignment groups, Canvas calculates assignment grades based on the associated percentage rather than treating all assignments as equally important. An example:

ScenarioGradeWhat It Reflects
Assignment groups weighted (exam = 60%)68 (D+)Accurately reflects poor performance on the most important assignment
Assignment groups unweighted86 (B)Inaccurately inflates grade; treats all assignments as equally important

Core Four — Item 3

No matter your age, educational level, or background, the first day of class can be nerve-wracking — for students and faculty alike. The first time a student opens your Canvas course is just like that first day of class, and it's critical for students to feel welcome in their new classroom environment. Posting a welcome message available on Day 1 is one of the easiest and most effective ways to set the tone for the semester.

SCC is a Caring Campus, dedicated to ensuring that our students experience a supportive, inclusive, and encouraging learning environment. One of the foundational pillars of the Caring Campus framework is its six behavioral commitments for faculty, including Commitment #1: Welcome students to the course and each course session.

We highly recommend using Canvas's Announcement tool to post welcome messages. Some useful things to include:

  • A brief description of the course and learning outcomes
  • Overall structure of the course
  • Textbook/Cengage/other required materials
  • Contact information
  • Canvas Instructor Guide — How do I add an announcement in a course?
  • Canvas Instructor Guide — How do I record a webcam video in Canvas Studio?
Core Four — Item 4

While we'd all love to have our courses fully built before the semester starts, we know this can be challenging. To that end, while we don't ask instructors to have a complete course ready on Day 1, we do ask that you have Week 1 content available for your students. We recommend:

  • Course syllabus addendum
  • Welcome message to students
  • "About Your Instructor" page
  • One low-stakes assignment to spur student engagement (introductions, syllabus quiz, etc.)

Week 1 content provides the essential foundation for your entire semester. It establishes academic momentum, aligns student expectations with instructor goals, and clarifies the relevance of the material. This also aligns with Caring Campus Commitment #5: Assignments Early and Often.

How Do I Check Items Off?

Checking items off the Instructor Checklist is a simple and streamlined process.

  1. Open ReadyGO Instructor in the course navigation
  2. Click on the Instructor Checklist on the main landing page to access the Core Four items
  3. A dynamic sidebar will appear with the Core Four items listed
  4. Simply toggle checklist items as you complete them — the total updates in real time

Screenshots showing the ReadyGO interface are available in your Canvas course shell.

Can I Create Custom Tasks?

While we don't currently allow instructors to create custom checklists, you can create custom tasks using the ReadyGO Instructor Tool to keep track of your own action items in addition to the Core Four.

  1. Go to Checklists & Tasks in the ReadyGO Instructor Tool
  2. Select + Course Task in either the top ribbon or the Course Tasks submenu
  3. Create a custom task for anything you'd like to keep track of
  4. A new Course Tasks checklist will appear on your dashboard, allowing you to check tasks off as you complete them

Screenshots showing this process are available in your Canvas course shell.
